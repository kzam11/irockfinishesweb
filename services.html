How to Choose

Polishing vs Honing vs Sealing — What's the Difference?

Each treatment serves a different purpose. Most restoration jobs combine two or three in sequence. Here's how to understand what your surface needs.

Treatment What it does When you need it Result Time
Honing Removes scratches, etch marks and surface damage using diamond abrasive pads Dull, scratched or etched marble, travertine or limestone Satin / matte Half–1 day
Polishing Follows honing — uses progressively finer grits to produce a high-gloss mirror finish After honing, or to restore gloss to a faded polished surface High gloss Half–1 day
Sealing Penetrating sealer fills pores to block stains, moisture and acid etching for up to 15 years After any restoration, or as standalone maintenance on porous stone and tile Protected 2–4 hours
Full Restoration Deep clean + hone + polish + seal — the complete process from damaged to flawless Neglected or heavily damaged surfaces that need to be brought back to new condition Like new 1–2 days
Non-Slip Coating Micro-etches the tile surface at a molecular level to increase traction without changing appearance Wet areas, commercial spaces, pool surrounds, stairs — anywhere slip risk is a concern Safer surface Half day

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does marble restoration take?

Most residential marble restoration jobs — including honing, polishing and sealing — are completed in a single day. Larger commercial projects or surfaces requiring extensive repair work may take two to three days. We always provide a clear timeframe before starting.

What is the difference between marble polishing and marble honing?

Honing removes surface scratches and etch marks using abrasive diamond pads, producing a smooth matte or satin finish. Polishing follows honing and uses progressively finer grits to bring the stone to a high-gloss, mirror-like shine. Most restoration projects include both steps in sequence. See our comparison table above for a full breakdown.

Is the sealer safe for children and pets?

Yes. Our professional-grade sealers are water-based and non-toxic once cured. We recommend keeping children and pets off treated surfaces for 24 hours while the sealer fully cures. After that, sealed surfaces are completely safe for everyday use.

Do I need to move furniture before the team arrives?

We ask that you clear the work area of small items, rugs and breakables. For large furniture such as sofas or dining tables, our team can assist with moving pieces as needed. We take care to protect surrounding surfaces and leave the space as we found it.

How much does marble restoration cost in Melbourne?

Cost depends on surface area, condition, stone type and treatment required. A single marble benchtop polish and seal typically starts from a few hundred dollars; larger floor restorations are priced per square metre.
