Why Seal Your Tiles

Sealing Protects Your Investment — and Makes Cleaning Effortless

Most tile damage is preventable. Grout lines are made from Portland cement — a porous material that absorbs spills, cooking oils, cleaning chemicals and foot traffic oils almost immediately after installation. Without a sealer, those contaminants bond permanently to the grout fibres and can't be removed, even with professional cleaning.

Natural stone tiles — terracotta, limestone, travertine, slate and sandstone — are similarly porous and will stain, discolour or etch without a penetrating sealer. Even polished granite, often assumed to be maintenance-free, benefits from sealing at grout lines.

We clean the tiles first (unsealed surfaces seal the contamination in, not out), apply the correct sealer for each tile type, and allow proper cure time. A job done properly the first time means you won't need to redo it for 5–15 years.

How We Do It

The Tile Sealing Process

01

Assessment

We test the existing sealer (if any), check porosity with a water drop test, and identify the tile species. This determines which product to use and whether cleaning is needed first.

02

Clean

An alkaline cleaner appropriate for the tile type removes existing contamination. Grout lines get a mechanical scrub to open the pores. The surface must be clean and dry before any sealer is applied.

03

Apply sealer

Penetrating sealer is applied generously, allowed to dwell until absorbed, then buffed off any surface excess before it cures. Two coats are standard on porous tiles.

04

Cure

2–4 hours for light foot traffic, 24 hours for full use, 48–72 hours for wet areas. We provide a written cure guide before we leave.

Common Questions

Tile Sealing FAQs

Does porcelain tile need sealing?

Most fully vitrified porcelain tiles have very low porosity and do not require sealing on the tile face. However, the grout between them almost always does — cement-based grout is highly porous and will stain permanently without a sealer. We assess each job on-site and recommend whether face sealing, grout sealing only, or both is needed.

How long does tile sealer last?

Penetrating sealers last 5–15 years on most tiles depending on traffic and cleaning products used. We use premium penetrating sealers and offer a 15-year warranty on our standard sealer range.

Can you seal tiles in showers and wet areas?

Yes. Wet area sealing — showers, pool surrounds, laundries — is one of our most common jobs. We use wet-area grade penetrating sealers that resist constant water exposure without trapping moisture beneath the surface.

How long after sealing can I use the tiles?

Most penetrating sealers cure in 2–4 hours for light foot traffic. We recommend waiting 24 hours before full use and avoiding wet areas for 48–72 hours. We provide specific cure times for each product used on the day.

Do you clean tiles before sealing?

Yes — always. Sealer applied over contamination locks the dirt in. We clean all surfaces with a professional-grade alkaline cleaner appropriate for the tile type, allow full dry time, then apply sealer. For grout lines, we use a grout-specific cleaner and often a mechanical scrub to open the pores first.
