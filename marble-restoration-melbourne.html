What We Do

Full Marble Restoration — From Dull and Damaged to Like New

Marble is calcium carbonate — a beautiful but chemically sensitive stone. Acidic substances like wine, lemon juice, vinegar and many household cleaners don't just stain marble, they chemically react with it, dissolving the polished surface and leaving dull etch marks that can't be cleaned away. Scratches from foot traffic and abrasive materials compound the damage over time.

We restore marble using industrial diamond abrasive systems — the same technology used to finish marble in European fabrication factories. Starting with coarser grits to remove deep scratches and etch marks, we work progressively finer until the stone achieves your chosen finish, then seal it with a penetrating sealer to protect against future damage.

Every surface is assessed individually before we start. Carrara behaves differently to Travertine, and a benchtop needs different handling to a high-traffic floor. We identify the stone, assess the damage level, and recommend the right treatment — then give you a clear quote before any work begins.

How We Do It

The Marble Restoration Process

01

Assessment

We identify the stone species, damage type (etch, scratch, stain or structural) and correct grit starting point. Never a one-size-fits-all approach.

02

Grinding (if required)

Deep scratches or lippage (uneven tiles) require coarse diamond grinding to level the surface. Most benchtops and lightly damaged floors skip this step.

03

Honing

Progressive diamond pads from 50 through to 400 grit remove etch marks and scratches, bringing the stone to a consistent matte or satin finish.

04

Polishing

800 to 3000 grit brings the stone to a mirror-like finish. We stop at the sheen level you've specified — honed, satin or full mirror.

05

Sealing

A penetrating sealer is applied to fill the stone's pores, blocking future stains and moisture. Our premium sealer range carries a 15-year warranty.

Common Questions

Marble Restoration FAQs

Can etch marks be removed from marble?

Yes. Etch marks are caused by acidic substances (wine, lemon juice, cleaning products) chemically reacting with marble's calcium carbonate composition. They can be fully removed by re-honing the affected area with diamond abrasive pads, then re-polishing to match the surrounding finish. The earlier you treat them, the easier the process.

How long does marble floor restoration last?

A professionally honed, polished and sealed marble floor holds its appearance for 3–7 years under normal residential use, and 1–3 years in high-traffic commercial environments. Our 15-year sealer warranty covers the penetrating sealer's stain resistance over that period.

What marble types do you restore?

We restore all marble varieties — Carrara, Calacatta, Travertine, Crema Marfil, Nero Marquina, Emperor Gold, Statuario and all other natural marble and limestone types. We assess each stone before recommending a treatment, as different marbles have different hardness and porosity levels.

Can you restore a marble benchtop without removing it?

Yes, in almost all cases. We restore marble benchtops in-situ — the stone stays installed. Our equipment is sized for kitchen and bathroom access, and we fully protect surrounding cabinetry, sinks and appliances during the process.

What finish options are available?

We offer honed (smooth matte), satin (soft sheen) and polished/mirror (high gloss). Most clients choose the finish matching the stone's original specification. We can also blend into existing surrounding areas if only part of the surface needs restoration.
