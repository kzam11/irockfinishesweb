CBD Commercial Specialists

Commercial Stone Restoration with Minimum Disruption

Melbourne's CBD has some of the highest concentrations of commercial natural stone in Australia — travertine lobby floors in Collins Street towers, marble reception areas in Bourke Street legal firms, polished granite retail entrances on Swanston Street. These surfaces are under constant foot traffic, and in a commercial environment, their condition directly reflects the business behind them.

We understand the constraints of CBD commercial work: access restrictions, building rules, OHS requirements, and the need to complete work without disrupting tenants, staff or customers. We offer after-hours and weekend scheduling for all CBD jobs, use wet-process equipment to manage dust, and arrive with a full methodology agreed in advance with building management.

We service the full inner-city commercial precinct — CBD, Docklands, Southbank, South Melbourne and the broader metropolitan area. Large commercial jobs are quoted on-site after a free inspection. Building managers and strata managers can contact us directly to arrange a schedule.

Common Questions

CBD Commercial Stone Restoration FAQs

Can you restore marble floors in a CBD office during business hours?

We can work during business hours in most cases, but prefer after-hours or weekend scheduling for CBD commercial jobs to avoid disruption to tenants and visitors. We use wet-process equipment that minimises dust, and work efficiently to reduce the footprint of each shift. We agree on a schedule with building management before starting.

Do you service Docklands and Southbank?

Yes. We service all of inner Melbourne's commercial precinct — CBD, Docklands, Southbank, South Melbourne and the broader metropolitan area. Large commercial jobs are quoted on-site after a free inspection.

How often does a commercial marble floor need restoration?

High-traffic commercial lobby floors typically need professional honing and polishing every 12–24 months depending on foot traffic volume and cleaning products used. Penetrating sealer retreatment is needed every 2–3 years. We can set up a maintenance schedule for building managers.
