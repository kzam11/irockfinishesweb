Stone Repair Melbourne

Colour-matched chip repair, crack filling and edge restoration for marble, granite, travertine and natural stone. Benchtops, floors and stairs across Melbourne and Victoria.

What We Do

Invisible Repairs — Not a Patch, a Restoration

A chip or crack in marble or stone doesn't have to mean replacement. Using two-part colour-matched polyester and epoxy fillers, we can repair most damage to a standard that's virtually invisible to the eye — even on close inspection. The filler is mixed and tinted on-site to match the stone's specific colour and veining pattern, applied and cured, then ground back flush and polished to the surrounding finish level.

The most common repairs we perform are edge chips on kitchen and bathroom benchtops — particularly at sink cutouts and near stovetops, where dropped pots and pans cause the most damage. Corner chips on floor tiles and cracks in shower bases are also extremely common, especially in older Melbourne properties where movement in the building substrate has caused stress fractures.

When the damage is too extensive for in-situ repair — a fully broken slab or a tile shattered beyond reconstruction — we'll tell you clearly, rather than attempt a repair that won't hold. We can also advise on the closest available replacement material if needed.

Repair or replace?

For most chips and cracks, repair costs a fraction of replacement and — done correctly — is undetectable. We recommend replacement only when the slab has structurally failed, or damage is too widespread to blend. We'll always be direct about which applies to your job.

Common Questions

Stone Repair FAQs

Can a chipped marble benchtop be repaired?

Yes. Most chips in marble benchtops can be repaired with colour-matched two-part epoxy filler. The chip is cleaned, the filler is mixed to match the stone's colour and veining, applied and cured, then ground back flush and polished to blend with the surrounding surface. The result is typically invisible on inspection.

Can you repair a crack in a marble floor tile?

Hairline cracks and minor fractures can be filled with a colour-matched filler and blended. Structural cracks where the tile has shifted require re-bonding the tile before filling. We assess each crack individually to recommend the right fix.

What causes chips in marble and stone?

The most common causes are: impact from dropped objects (particularly on benchtop edges and corners), installation damage, and point loading on unsupported overhangs. Edge chips on kitchen benchtops near the sink are especially common, as the edge is the thinnest and most exposed point.

Is it better to repair or replace a damaged stone benchtop?

For chips and cracks, repair is almost always the better option — typically a fraction of replacement cost and, when done well, undetectable. Replacement only makes more sense for structural slab failures or when the client wants a completely different stone.
