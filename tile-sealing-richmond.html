Tile Sealing for Richmond's Mix of Old and New Properties

Richmond has one of Melbourne's most diverse property mixes — Victorian-era terrace homes with original slate and terracotta tiles sitting alongside new-build apartments and converted commercial spaces. Each property type has different tile sealing needs, and we've worked across all of them.

In Richmond's older terrace homes, the most common jobs are: deep-cleaning and sealing stained terracotta or slate floors that have never been properly sealed, restoring old grout lines that are discoloured from years of foot traffic and cleaning products, and sealing bathroom tiles in kitchens and wet areas that have been renovated without proper sealing.

In Richmond's commercial strip — Church Street, Swan Street and Bridge Road — we work on restaurant and café floors, hospitality spaces and retail stores where tile hygiene and appearance is part of the business.

Common Questions

Richmond Tile Sealing FAQs

Do you seal tiles in Richmond terrace homes?

Yes. Richmond's Victorian terrace homes are a common job for us — original slate, bluestone and terracotta tiles that haven't been sealed in years, or older grout lines that have stained and need deep cleaning before sealing. We work in all property types from historic terraces to new-build apartments.

Can you restore old terracotta tiles in a Richmond terrace?

Yes. Old terracotta tiles are extremely porous and absorb stains deeply if left unsealed. We strip the existing contamination with a professional alkaline cleaner, scrub grout lines, allow full dry time, then apply two coats of penetrating sealer. We can also enhance the colour of terracotta with an enhancing sealer to bring out the natural red tones.

How much does tile sealing in Richmond cost?

Tile sealing is priced per square metre, with most residential jobs in the $200–$800 range depending on area size and tile type. Commercial jobs are priced on inspection. We provide a fixed written quote before starting — no surprises on the day.
