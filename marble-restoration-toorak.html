Toorak Marble Specialists

Luxury Marble Restoration for Toorak's Finest Homes

Toorak has some of Melbourne's highest concentrations of natural marble — large-format Carrara and Calacatta floors in grand entrances, Italian marble benchtops in renovated kitchens, travertine staircases and stone bathroom suites. These surfaces represent significant investment, and maintaining or restoring them properly requires specialist knowledge of each stone's characteristics.

We work in Toorak regularly and understand the expectations that come with high-end residential work — punctuality, clean and careful work practice, full protection of surrounding surfaces, and results that justify the property's calibre. We also understand the stones: the difference between genuine Carrara and Calacatta Gold in terms of hardness and polishing behaviour, and how to achieve a mirror-level finish without over-grinding.

If your marble has become dull, etched from acidic cleaning products, or scratched from foot traffic, we can return it to its original condition — or better. We offer honed, satin and polished finishes, and can match the existing standard if only part of the surface needs treatment.

Common Questions

Toorak Marble Restoration FAQs

Do you service Toorak for marble restoration?

Yes. Toorak is one of our most frequent service areas. We regularly restore marble floors, staircases and benchtops in Toorak homes and apartment buildings. We service all of inner east Melbourne including Toorak, South Yarra, Hawthorn, Armadale, Malvern and Richmond.

How do you protect our home during marble restoration?

We lay down full surface protection before beginning — floor coverings in adjacent rooms, plastic sheeting on cabinetry and joinery within the work area, and dust containment for grinding work. We treat every property carefully, and our team works cleanly and removes all waste on completion.

Can you match the finish if only part of the floor needs work?

Yes, in most cases. We assess the existing finish and match our honing and polishing sequence to achieve the same sheen across the whole surface. For localised etch marks or scratches, we can often treat just the affected area and blend it to match, rather than doing the entire floor.
