Concrete Sealing Melbourne

Professional concrete grinding, honing, polishing and sealing for driveways, patios, warehouse floors and polished concrete interiors across Melbourne and Victoria.

What We Do

From Dull and Stained to Smooth, Sealed and Protected

Concrete is one of the most durable flooring materials available — but without the right treatment it's also one of the hardest to maintain. Unsealed concrete absorbs oil from vehicles, food from kitchen spills and moisture from Melbourne's weather within minutes of contact. Those stains go deep and are almost impossible to remove without diamond grinding.

We work with concrete across the full range — from grinding out stains and surface damage on a driveway, through to achieving a full polished concrete finish for a residential open-plan floor or commercial lobby. The process is the same; the number of grit stages determines the final finish level.

We also apply concrete densifiers before sealing — a lithium or sodium silicate product that chemically hardens the surface and increases abrasion resistance. Most DIY concrete sealing skips this step, which is why those jobs need redoing every 12–18 months.

Common Questions

Concrete Sealing FAQs

What is the difference between honed and polished concrete?

Honed concrete is ground to a smooth matte finish — typically 200–800 grit — with a consistent flat appearance and no reflection. Polished concrete continues to 1500 or 3000 grit, creating a semi-gloss to mirror-like surface. Honed is most common in residential living areas; polished suits commercial lobbies and showrooms.

Does concrete need sealing after grinding or polishing?

Always. Unsealed concrete is highly porous and stains almost immediately. After grinding or polishing we apply a penetrating densifier (which hardens the surface) followed by a guard/sealer. This gives stain resistance and determines the final sheen level.

Can you remove oil stains from a driveway?

In most cases, yes. Oil stains, paint, efflorescence and surface discolouration can be removed through diamond grinding followed by sealing. Significant cracking or spalling may require filling before grinding. We assess the driveway on-site before recommending the right approach.

How long does concrete sealing last?

Penetrating concrete sealers last 3–7 years depending on traffic and exposure. Commercial driveways under regular vehicle traffic may need retreatment every 2–3 years. We advise on the right maintenance schedule for each job.
